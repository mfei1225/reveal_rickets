Case 8: Why are my daughters legs bent?

Michael Fei

A 3-year-old girl is brought to the ED because she has “pain in her bones and feels weak”. On physical examination, she has tenderness to palpation of both wrists. She has a waddling gait and bowed legs. X-rays reveal lucency of the bones, physeal widening and metaphyseal cupping. Laboratory testing reveals decreased serum calcium, phosphate and vitamin D levels, increased alkaline phosphatase and parathyroid hormone levels. Describe the most likely diagnosis and review the pathophysiology.

Rickets vs Osteomalacia

  • Rickets is deficient bone mineralization of growth plate (pediatric)
  • Osteomalacia is deficient bone mineralization of bone matrix (adults)

Etiology

  • Inbalance in calcium and phosphate levels
  • Calcipenic and Phosphopenic

Calcipenic

  • Deficiency of vitamin D (diet, sun exposure, malabsorption)
  • Nutritional deficiency of calcium
  • Mutation in 1-alpha-hydroxylase enzyme(CYP27B1 gene)
  • Mutation in vitamin D receptor(VDR gene)
    • ↓ Vit D → ↓ Calcium → ↑ PTH → ↑ Bone Reabsorption → Weak Bones

Phosphopenic

  • Hereditary hypophosphatemic rickets (failure in phosphate reabsorption)
  • Fanconi syndrome (failure in phosphate reabsorption)
    • ↓ Phosphate → ↓ Osteoblast and Osteoclast function → Weak Bones

Labs Summary

Condition Serum Ca2+ PO ALP PTH
calcipenic ↓ ↓ ↑ ↑
phosphopenic normal ↓ ↓ normal or ↑

Clinical Presentations

  • Leg bowing
  • Cupping in metaphysis
  • Pectus carinatum
  • Rachitic rosary
